The Human Factor



Engineers will tell you that the most failure-prone part of any machine is the part which is most complex. In an aircraft, the most complicated part is the minds of the people who operate it.

In my videos, I use my knowledge of Psychology to shed light on the reasons behind aircraft accidents, which almost always involve this so-called ‘human factor’.

Along with incredible improvements in avionics (aviation electronics) over the past few decades, the biggest improvements to aviation safety have been the result of a better understanding of the role that Psychology plays in the cockpit.


Innovations such as Crew Resource Management have revolutionised the way pilots are trained, and the way that they practise day-to-day. This has made flying much safer than it was even just 30 or 40 years ago.

The aim of this channel is to highlight these changes, and to show where the industry is going as a result.
