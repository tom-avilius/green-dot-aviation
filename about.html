About Me



Hi!

Emmet here, aka ‘Green Dot’. I started this YouTube channel in 2021 as a way of combining my passion for aviation and my fascination with Psychology.

My interest in aviation began with a toy plane I received as a present when I was 5 years old. I would try to peer through the painted-on cockpit windows, and imagine that behind them, there must be 8 people frantically flicking switches and manipulating levers trying to get the massive machine in the air. How else could a machine so big and unwieldy fly?

Now in my 20s, my understanding of what goes on in the cockpit has changed, but flying retains that same sense of magic and wonder to me. There is something miraculous about the fact that it’s possible at all. And by humans!

Hence my other great interest -- Psychology. How is it that such a plain-looking organ as the brain, can give rise to the sheer variety of behaviours we see in people, let alone to conscious awareness?

To satisfy this curiosity, I studied Psychology in university, getting both a Bachelor’s and a Master’s degree in the subject.

I enjoyed this immensely, but, as it turned out, I joined Psychology too early in its history to get the answers to all of my questions (but I did learn how to frame the questions better!).

After university, I worked as a researcher in the field of Educational Psychology. The work was interesting, but I still had an itch to do something in aviation. As a result, I created Green Dot Aviation in late 2021, making videos which focus on the psychological side of aviation accidents and incidents.

Since then, it’s become a full-time job, and I’m delighted that all sorts of people, from pilots to those who aren’t even interested in aviation enjoy the videos and find them educational.

I love what I do, and I am incredibly grateful for the opportunities that this channel and its supporters have given me.

I am now learning to fly, and am most of the way towards getting my Private Pilot’s License, with the aim of one day fulfilling my childhood dream of being an airline pilot.

I hope you enjoy these videos and learn something from them. I am lucky enough to have grown a (very talented) team to make them with me, and our aim is to keep improving them for you, so that we produce the highest-quality videos of their kind on the internet.
